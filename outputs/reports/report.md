# 📊 Telco Customer Churn Analysis — Final Report

## 🎯 Objective

The goal of this analysis is to understand the key factors driving customer churn and provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention.


## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Early Customer Churn

Customers with low tenure (especially within the first 12 months) show the highest churn rates.

### 2. Contract Type Impact

Month-to-month contracts are strongly associated with higher churn compared to long-term contracts.

### 3. Pricing Sensitivity

Customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to churn, indicating price sensitivity.

### 4. High-Risk Segment

Customers with:

* Low tenure
* High monthly charges

represent the highest risk group.

### 5. High-Value Customers

Customers with high total charges contribute significantly to revenue but also show notable churn risk.


## 📊 Business Implications

* Customer churn is concentrated in the **early lifecycle stage**
* Pricing and contract flexibility significantly influence retention
* High-value customers require targeted retention strategies


## 🚀 Recommendations

### 1. Improve Onboarding

Enhance customer experience during the first 12 months to reduce early churn.

### 2. Promote Long-Term Contracts

Offer incentives or discounts to encourage customers to switch from month-to-month to long-term plans.

### 3. Pricing Optimization

Review pricing strategies for customers with high monthly charges.

### 4. Target High-Risk Customers

Develop targeted campaigns for customers identified as high risk.

### 5. Retain High-Value Customers

Provide personalized offers and support for high-value customers to reduce potential revenue loss.


## 🧠 Conclusion

This analysis highlights that churn is influenced by a combination of customer lifecycle stage, pricing, and contract type.
By focusing on early engagement and targeted retention strategies, the company can significantly reduce churn and improve customer lifetime value.
